Welcome to the official webpage of the Hackathon of the Machine Learning Organization at IIT

The Hackathon will take place on April 4th starting at 9:00 am local time, at the Illinois Institute of Technology, at Chicago. The precise location to be announced.


We plan to use the R software in order to program a Random Forest that can predict the winner of the 2020 US presidential elections. We are currently looking for good data sets that can contain enough information to make reasonable predictions. If we fail to find convincing databases for this purpose, we may switch the hackathon and orientate it to predict the emotions of people from a picture of your face.


If you want to learn what is a Random Tree we recommend to read the book CART by Leo Breiman et al. If you wish, one of our members has made a 30min video about a Random Forest. In the first 15 minutes he explains the basics of a classification tree. We also attach a description of the Hackathon programming content together with several of the basic R commands that we may use.
